# Naqel Inspection Intelligence System
---

## Description
This project is an Enterprise-Grade Intelligence Engine designed for leading Ethiopian coffee exporter.  

It analyzes 2,300 high-fidelity inspection records to bridge the gap between warehouse quality control and financial profitability.  

The system automates the grading of coffee lots based on:
- ECTA (Ethiopian Coffee and Tea Authority) standards
- Naqel (CLU) export inspection standards

This provides real-time visibility into export readiness and operational performance.

---

## The Problem: The $1.05M Inventory Block

Romina PLC faced a major operational bottleneck:

- 17.6% of total inventory (404 lots) failed Naqel export inspection  
- $1,050,000.00 in capital was trapped in rejected inventory  

### Key Challenges
- **Lack of Visibility**  
  Inability to distinguish:
  - Total Losses (physical defects)  
  - Near-Misses (moisture issues)

- **Operational Inefficiency**  
  Poor resource allocation due to unclear failure drivers  

- **High Volatility**  
  Quality instability at washing stations (Astatke Menafesha) disrupted the:
  - 45-container weekly export cadence

---

## The Solution: Predictive Compliance & Recovery Modeling

A Three-Phase Intelligence System was developed to transform raw inspection data into actionable strategy:

### 1. Compliance Engine
- Automated grading logic for:
  - Grade 1 (Specialty)
  - Grade 2 (Premium)
- Instantly flags non-compliant lots

### 2. Forensic Quality Audit
- Conducted multivariate correlation analysis  
- Key insight:
  - Primary defects (-0.535 correlation) are the main driver of rejection  
  - NOT farm altitude

### 3. The $215K Recovery Model
- Built a "Rescue Filter"
- Identified:
  - 83 high-quality lots failing only moisture criteria  
- Recovery opportunity:
  - $215,717.83 in blocked capital

---

## Strategic Recommendations

Based on Deep Audit:

### Targeted Re-Drying
- Re-process the 83 "Near-Miss" lots  
- Achieve < 11.5% moisture threshold  
- Unlock $215K revenue

### Station Stabilization
- Deploy technical supervisors to:
  - Astatke Menafesha  
  - Zenebe Dikicha  
- Reduce 3.85% Coefficient of Variation (CV)

### Training Shift
- Reallocate investment:
  - Less focus on high-altitude sourcing  
  - More focus on station labor training  

**Insight:**  
Physical sorting defects are 3× more damaging than other quality factors.
